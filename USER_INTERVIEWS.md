# USER_INTERVIEWS

## Interview 1 — Om Bhise

**Role:** Software Engineer  
**Company Stage:** Mid-sized product/service company  
**Interview Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Interview Type:** Informal chat conversation

### Conversation Summary

Om currently uses mostly free AI tooling including ChatGPT, Gemini, and GitHub Copilot. He does not personally pay for subscriptions right now, which immediately challenged one assumption behind Spendora: not all developers are willing to pay for premium AI tooling unless the perceived value is very obvious.

The conversation shifted from “subscription management” into “subscription justification.” Om repeatedly emphasized that many AI tools feel expensive relative to their real-world usefulness. He specifically mentioned frustration around token limits and inconsistent reliability.

The interview also surfaced an important visibility issue. Even though he personally does not manage team subscriptions, he believed most teams likely would not know their total AI spending unless someone intentionally tracked it.

### Direct Quotes

> “I mostly use free versions of AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot.”

> “Many AI subscriptions don’t feel worth the price.”

> “Token limits are lower than expected, and in many cases the tools are not efficient or reliable enough for the cost.”

> “I don’t think most people would actually know the total monthly AI spending unless it’s tracked properly.”

### Most Surprising Insight

The most surprising part of this interview was that the problem was not only duplicate subscriptions or fragmented tooling. The bigger frustration was perceived value inefficiency — developers questioning whether premium AI subscriptions actually justify their pricing.

### What Changed About the Product

This conversation pushed Spendora beyond simple “cost-cutting.” It reinforced the importance of:
- highlighting inefficient subscriptions
- evaluating value-to-cost ratio
- supporting mixed free + paid AI environments
- making spending visibility understandable at a team level

## Interview 2 — Jyoti Kachave

**Role:** Technical Web Content Writer  
**Company:** MilesWeb Internet Services Pvt Ltd  
**Company Stage:** Established hosting and internet services company  
**Interview Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Interview Type:** Messaging conversation

### Conversation Summary

Jyoti described a much broader AI tooling environment than expected. Different departments inside the company use different AI tools depending on workflow needs. Her team regularly interacts with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Canva AI, GitHub Copilot, Grammarly, and multiple SEO/automation tools.

One of the strongest insights from this interview was how fragmented AI tooling becomes across departments. Some subscriptions are centrally managed while others are purchased independently by employees or teams. That creates visibility gaps where nobody has a single complete picture of company-wide AI usage or spending.

Interestingly, she also highlighted an organizational asymmetry: employees often do not know whether overlapping subscriptions exist because access and management responsibilities are distributed.

### Direct Quotes

> “Our team regularly uses tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Canva AI, GitHub Copilot, Grammarly, and a few AI-powered SEO and automation tools.”

> “Subscriptions are a mix of centrally managed and individually purchased tools.”

> “AI spending is tracked, but it’s still somewhat fragmented across departments.”

> “The biggest improvement would be having a centralized system for AI tool visibility, usage tracking, and license management.”

### Most Surprising Insight

The most surprising realization was that even companies already tracking AI spending can still lack centralized visibility. Tracking existed, but operational understanding remained fragmented across departments.

### What Changed About the Product

This interview reinforced the need for:
- centralized visibility dashboards
- cross-department AI spend aggregation
- license management tooling
- organization-wide usage reporting
- operational rather than purely financial optimization

It also strengthened the idea that Spendora should eventually support team-level visibility instead of focusing only on individuals.

## Interview 3 — Yash Thakare

**Role:** Founder  
**Company:** ShorShield  
**Company Stage:** Early-stage startup  
**Interview Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Interview Type:** Founder interview via messaging

### Conversation Summary

Yash currently manages AI subscriptions directly with his co-founder. Their tooling stack includes Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, Emergent, and Antigravity. Since the team is still very small, AI subscriptions are manually managed without formal systems.

Unlike larger organizations, overlapping subscriptions were not yet a major problem. However, he immediately pointed out that this would likely become harder as the company grows.

One particularly important insight was that even very small startups already experience light subscription waste. He admitted that unused subscriptions occasionally remain active simply because management is manual.

The conversation also introduced an interesting platform-level idea: a centralized AI tooling marketplace where subscriptions, tracking, and management all exist together.

### Direct Quotes

> “As of now our team is only me and my co-founder thus we manage the subscriptions ourselves.”

> “Not really for us, didn’t happen so far, but might happen when we grow in team size.”

> “Yes we track it manually.”

> “Maybe a centralised platform that has all the AI tools at a place, where we can buy subscriptions, track them.”

### Most Surprising Insight

The most surprising insight was that even a two-person startup already experiences operational AI subscription friction. The problem appears much earlier than expected — before formal finance or procurement systems even exist.

### What Changed About the Product

This conversation changed the positioning of Spendora significantly:
- Spendora should not only target large companies
- small startups already experience fragmented AI tooling management
- lightweight tracking and visibility may matter before advanced analytics
- future growth complexity is itself a strong selling point

This interview also introduced the idea that Spendora could evolve beyond optimization into a centralized AI operations platform in the future.
